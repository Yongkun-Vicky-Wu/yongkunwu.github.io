“Your Body is a Battleground”: A Resurrection of Body and Affect
Miriam Cahn
Oct 01 2021 - Jan 01 2022
The Power Plant, 231 Queens Quay West, Toronto, Ontario M5J 2G8 Canada
“Using your body as an instrument... We have to put pictorial gesture behind us — it’s hopeless, really hopeless. It serves no purpose. It’s the whole body that is the instrument, and the result is what it is.”
—Miriam Cahn

Swiss artist Miriam Cahn’s exhibition, “ME AS HAPPENING” (2021-2022), presents works over a span of twenty-five years. The body is central to her personal and political expressions in relation to gender, migration, displacement, terrorism, and the events of 9/11. Situated in a context charged with political turmoil and gender/sexuality struggles, Cahn’s works are expressive, powerful, and even confrontational. This article explores how the deployment of the body could be theorized with Brian Massumi’s philosophy of body and affect.

瑞士艺术家Miriam Cahn的展览ME AS HAPPENING（2021-2022 年）展示了她二十五年来的作品。身体是她个人和政治表达的核心，其中涉及性别、移民、流离失所、恐怖主义和 9/11 事件。在充满政治动荡和性别/性斗争的背景下，Cahn的作品极具表现力、震撼力甚至对抗性。基于Brian Massumi的身体与情动（body and affect）哲学，本文尝试对Cahn艺术作品中对身体意象的运用进行理论化。

Cultural studies have long emphasized social context, positionality, system, and power. Visual/literary works, according to poststructuralist theories, are approached as representations of the precoded sociopolitical reality. The Brillo pads and cans of soup Andy Warhol reproduced are not household objects in themselves, but always representations of the capitalist consumer society. Built on existing cultural studies, Massumi proposes a rethinking of the body, or more broadly, the material culture; “let matter be matter, brains be brains, jellyfish be jellyfish... in irreducible alterity.” By a resurrection of the body, Massumi has no intention of contradicting the established poststructuralist concepts, but to “refresh their vocabulary with conceptual infusions from neglected sources or underappreciated aspects of known sources.”

长期以来，文化研究一直强调社会背景、地位、制度和权力。根据后结构主义理论，视觉/文学作品被视为预先设定的社会政治现实的代表。Andy Warhol在他的作品中再现的布里洛盒子和金宝汤罐头并不是生活用品本身，而始终是资本主义消费社会的表征。在现有文化研究的基础上，Massumi提出了对身体的重新思考，或者更广泛地说，对物质文化的重新思考；“让物质成为物质，大脑成为大脑，水母成为水母......存留在在不可化归的绝对他性中"。Massumi将身体概念复兴的行为无意与既有的后结构主义概念相对抗，他希望“通过将概念加入被忽视或已知的来源来刷新它们现有的话语”。

Body and Motion

The body has the potential to resist not by merely signifying revolt, that is, being precoded on the grid of resistance, but by constantly being in motion, constantly “becoming” rather than “being.” According to Massumi, “positionality is an emergent quality of movement” and that position is secondary to movement. The oftentimes glowing naked bodily figures in Cahn's works exemplify the body in motion/passage. It bears intertextuality with Wassily Kandinsky’s “halo outline” that signifies spiritual energy or cosmic symbolism. Rather than a demarcation, the glowing outlines represent a constant interaction of the body and the environment; it is a non-static relation constantly in motion.

身体具有反抗的潜能，这不仅是通过“标志”反抗，即被预先设定在反抗的结构上，而是同时通过不断地运动（in motion），不断地“成为”（becoming）而不是“存在”（being）。Massumi认为，“位置性（positionality）产生于运动（movement）”，位置对于运动来说是次要的。Cahn作品中时常发光的裸体人物体现了运动中的身体。它与Wassily Kandinsky的“光晕轮廓”（halo outline）具有互文性，后者象征着精神能量或宇宙的意义。发光的轮廓并不是一种分界线，而是代表了身体与环境的不断互动；这是一种不断运动的非静态关系。

“The fact that with every move, with every change, there is something new to the world, an added reality. The world is self-augmenting. Reality ‘snowballs’...” As Cahn creates artworks that depict genitals and physical details with exceptional eruptive energy, she is disrupting the existing heteronormativity (traditional women roles, simplified female representations, etc.) and augmenting; she is “unmaking sense by scrambling significations already in place,” giving birth to new emergences.

“事实上，每一次移动，每一次变化，都会给世界带来一些新的东西，一种新的现实。世界会自我增强。现实会‘滚雪球’......” Cahn创作的艺术作品以非凡的爆发力描绘生殖器和身体细节，她正在打破现有的异性恋规范（传统的女性角色、简化的女性形象等）并进行对女性表达的扩增；她正在“通过扰乱已有的意义来创造意义”，催生新的出现。

Cahn’s resistance to attach any precoded, qualified meaning to the work is also exemplary in her remarks on flowers, “[A flower] is a flower, not a symbol... everyone sees what they want to see. If you want to see it as a symbol of humanity, that’s your business. It’s your own thoughts that make you say that. For me, a flower is a flower, and it stays a flower. In any case, it’s not a symbol of something else, or an idea. That’s a lousy idea. It’s always a bit complicated to look at art like that because it tries to define something that is not defined, and that you can’t define, and that shouldn’t be defined.” The same could apply to her frequent depiction of body, the “sucking, sleeping, penetrating, fisting, punching” bodies that are always open to spectators’ interpretations.

Cahn拒绝为作品附加任何预先设定的意义，这一点在她关于花的评论中也体现得淋漓尽致：“[花]就是花，不是象征......每个人都会看到自己想看的东西。如果你想把它看作人类的象征，那是你的事。是你自己的想法让你这么说的。对我来说，花就是花，它就是花。无论如何，它都不是其他东西或思想的象征。这是个糟糕的想法。看这样的艺术总是有点复杂，因为它试图定义一些没有定义的东西，你无法定义，也不应该定义。”这同样适用于她对身体的频繁描绘。“吸吮的、沉睡的、插入的、狂躁的”身体总是可以被观众以各种方式解读。

Emergence and the Emerged

Of course, the depicted body or other natural objects are by no means neutral and innocent. The body, not of the artworks, but of the artist herself, “infolds context [and] volitions and cognitions that are nothing if not situated.” But still, the body as an instrument to initiate actions should reclaim its critical role. Social context (in Massumi’s words, content/form or qualifications) is inseparable from the body (natural, mechanic bodily workings). This, according to Massumi, can be explained in terms of the distinction between conditions of emergence and re-conditionings of the emerged. “Conditions of emergence are one with becoming. Re-conditonings of the emerged define normative or regulatory operations that set the parameters of history... But if feedback from the dimension of the emerged re-conditions the conditions of emergence, then it also has to be recognized that conditions of emergence change. Emergence emerges. Changing changes.” In other words, qualifications (the emerged, the secondary) could in turn shape the emergence, the primary.

当然，被描绘的身体或其他自然物体绝不是中立和无辜的。身体，并非艺术作品的身体，而是艺术家本人的身体，“蕴含着语境[和]意志与认知，如果没有语境，它就什么也不是”。但是，物质的身体作为行为发起者，仍应重新发挥其关键作用。社会语境与身体（自然的、机械的身体运作）密不可分。Massumi认为，这可以用“涌现的条件”（conditions of emergence）和“被涌现者的再条件”（re-conditionings of the emerged）之间的区别来解释。“涌现的条件与成为是一体的... 但是，如果涌现导致的反馈（feedback）重新设定了涌现的条件，那么也必须承认涌现的条件是变化的。新涌现的涌现。变化的变化”。

If bodies in Cahn’s works are left undefined or in a state of “becoming” in the first place, spectators’ interpretations involving sociohistorical testimony could change the initial emergence. The vibrant strokes and devastating bodies in Cahn’s works seem to be shouting at the viewers, “Stop defining me with any societal or historical associations in the first place! Look and feel the intensity and how your own body is affected.” The primacy of the emergence, the one with becoming, thus inevitably leads to the problem of affect and intensity as the postmodern world of ideology gradually gives way to affect.

如果Cahn作品中的身体一开始没有被定义或处于“成为”的状态，那么观众涉及社会历史的解释可能会改变其最初的涌现。Cahn作品中充满活力的笔触和极具破坏性的身体似乎在向观众大喊：“首先，不要再用任何社会或历史关联来定义我！看一看，感受一下这种强度（intensity）以及你自己的身体是如何受到影响的（affect）。”因此，随着后现代世界的意识形态逐渐让位于情感，“涌现”的首要地位不可避免地促成了情感（affect）和强度（intensity）的问题。

Affect and Intensity

“Body connotes the potential to affect or to be affected, and the degree is intensity.” Although intensity is mostly concerned with automatic reactions on the skin surface, it is not presocial. Intensity combines the social in a logic unperceived by consciousness. The reason I highlight the problem of affect/intensity (or present purposes, affect and intensity point to the same thing) here is to suggest spectators with a less contextual way of looking. This is not to undermine the role of constructivism but to bring the oftentimes neglected affect/intensity into conversation. “Integrating intensity into the event that favors structure, in which ‘all eventual permutations are prefigured,” not only provides audiences with new viewing methodologies and experiences, but also releases new potential for emergence.

“身体意味着影响或被影响的潜力，而程度则是强度。”虽然强度主要涉及皮肤表面的自动反应，但它并不具有社会性。强度以一种意识无法感知的逻辑结合了社会性。我之所以在此强调影响/强度（affect/intensity）问题（或现在的目的，影响和强度指向同一事物），是为了向观众建议一种去语境的观看方式。这并不是要削弱建构主义的作用，而是要将时常被忽视的情感/强度问题引入讨论。将“强度”融入偏重结构的活动中，这不仅为观众提供了新的观看方法和体验，也释放了新的涌现潜力。

Coda

As the title “ME AS HAPPENING” suggests, the physicality of bodies, both spectators’ and art figures’ bodies, should be regarded as expression events that spatialize and embody reality. Do not let the social construct override the initiating potential of the body might be our only way to regain a sense of control in this digitally disorienting world.

正如标题ME AS HAPPENING所暗示的那样，身体的物理性，无论是观众的身体还是艺术作品中人物的身体，都应被视为空间化和身体化的表达事件。不要让社会建构凌驾于身体的涌动潜能之上，这可能是我们在这个数字迷失的世界中重新获得控制感的唯一途径。

En Creux: The Audiovisual Objects

“En Creux: The Audiovisual Objects” is a sound visualization project based on the application of a piano and TouchDesigner, a node-based visual programming language for interactive multimedia content. “En_creux” (English: hollowed out), the French neologism I use to descriptively capture the core of this project, was originally coined by French music theorist Michel Chion. According to Chion, the attempt “to create a purposeful and fruitful tension between what is on screen and what is kindled in the mind of the audience” creates an in-between tension, which he terms “en creux”. [ Michel Chion, Audio-vision: sound on screen. Columbia University Press, (2019): xix.] In this project, I create audio-reactive geometric shapes based on several clips of Ludwig Van Beethoven’s world-class sonatas Moonlight and Pathétique. The en creux aspect precisely lies in the insurmountable gap between what is presented on the screen by TouchDesigner and audiences’ imagination. Beethoven’s Moonlight and Pathétique are chosen as major sound sources for their unprecedentedly high-contrast rhythms. The highly energetic pieces could easily be visualized and audiences could easily feel the changing flow of sound.

Theoretical Framing

The idea of rendering new media art that combines visual art and sound originally derives from Michel Chion’s concept of “synchresis”. Along with the concept of “the audiovisual contract”, “synchresis” suggests “the forging of an immediate and necessary relationship between something one sees and something one hears at the same time”.[ Chion, Audio-vision: sound on screen. Columbia University Press, (2019): 224.] In other words, sound and visual arts form a whole instead of “added” supplements to one another, creating the audiovisual objects.

“Added value” is a concept Michel Chion further proposes to theorize the complex relationship of sound and image. He defined it as “the expressive and informative value with which a sound enriches a given image so as to create the definitive impression”.[ Ibid, 5.] In short, added value naturalizes the combination of sound and image and facilitates the reciprocal interaction of the two entities. Sound and images convey drastically different meanings and feelings when appear independently and ensemble.

In addition, the concept of “intermedia characteristics” proposed by Föllmer et al. is enlightening.[ Föllmer et al, Audiovisions. Music as an Intermedia Art Form, 1931, quoted in Nuno Correia, Intermedia audiovisual objects, School of Arts, Design and Architecture, (2013): 58.] The theory is highly correlated with what new media artist Nuno Correia called the “inherent multi-sensoriality in music”, which indicates that the encounter and confrontation of different media are embedded in music.[ Ibid, 58.] Similarly, Föllmer et al. highlight the fact that music is inscribed in the visual environment, whether through “special production and reception conditions” or “graphic symbols and visually influenced forms of interaction”.[ Ibid, 58.] The notion of interaction, especially that of the visual and the audio, is key to the theory.

About TouchDesigner

As a node-based visual programming language, TouchDesigner enables multiple built-in functions, with which multimedia works can be realized. Nodes and operators, which refer to the fundamental visual blocks, are pieces of software that take inputs and produce outputs. Divided into six categories, operators from the same block family can be connected to generate outputs. To view the final work in motion, users can select “Perform Mode”, in which the default background color is black.[ Markku Laskujärvi, “Real-time interactive animated visualisations for music performances: comparing Blender game engine and TouchDesigner,” (2018): 14-18.] In “En Creux: The Audiovisual Objects”, the CHOP and TOP families are commonly used to be merged into pipelines that ultimately enable the rendered geometries to respond to sounds.

Tangible Crystal, Scattered Dots, and Musical Strings

The project is composed of “tangible crystal”, “scattered dots”, and “musical strings”, which respectively refer to the basic geometries of a 3D sphere, 2D circles, and straight lines. As a novice in TouchDesigner, I could not foresee the ultimate changing pattern of the shapes as I render the geometries, adding a sense of spontaneity to the works. CHOPs, TOPs, and other components are commonly used to input audio and shapes. I recorded the transfiguration of the geometries that are reactive to the Beethoven sonatas in the TouchDesigner performance mode. On the website, the works are displayed in the form of videos with background music.

Conclusion

“En_Creux: The Audiovisual Objects” is an experimental project that aims to renew listener-viewers’ audiovisual experiences. TouchDesigner enables the combination of visual arts and music, achieving “synchresis” and presenting the “added value” of the two realms. The ultimate success of the combination is open to the audiences’ judge. In the first place, classical music composed by Beethoven could bring confusion to any untrained ear in the professional area. Visualizing sonatas in a geometrical way, this audiovisual project can be seen as an attempt to reduce audiences’ distance from the “grandiose” classics.
